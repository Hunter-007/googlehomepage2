The Forgotten Man

Tell me what can compare to a father's love

Unrequited, never ending

Shouldering responsibility and blames alike

With no hope nor desire for reward in sight

Tell me, what can compate to a father's toil

Day and night in the fields to satisfy your wants

and needs, paying no attention to his

Nor to the sore felt in his achilles

Tell me what can compare to a fathers bond

Ever present, yet never suffocating

Channeling pain from the child in his care

and making it his burden to bear

Tell em what can compare to a father's joy

Seeing you become greater than he ever wants

A life of servitude with your success as rewards

His smile overshadowing pain that goes beyond words

Tell me what can compare to a father's death

A loss incomparable, a thought too painful to bear

A child inconsolable, a mother in a puddle of tears

Another father gone, another family left in despair

An ode to all fathers - June 2021
